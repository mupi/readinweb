4. Os dois trechos que seguem veiculam a mesma informação. Analise qual das duas versões facilita mais a leitura.


a) - Long ago, in 1613, in India, there lived an emperor who loved the beautiful empress so dearly that the emperor built a wonderful mausoleum for the empress so that the empress would be remembered forever. The empress's name was Mumtaz-i-Mahal, which meant “Crown of the Palace”. The emperor and the empress lived happily for 18 years and had 13 children. Unfortunately, the empress died giving birth to the emperor and the empress”s 14th child. The emperor was so grieved because the empress had died that the emperor built the tomb in the memory of the empress.
b) - Long ago, in India, in 1613, there lived an emperor who loved his beautiful empress so dearly that he built a wonderful mausoleum for her so that she would be remembered forever. Her name was Mumtaz-i-Mahal, which meant “Crown of the Palace”. The couple lived happily for 18 years and had 13 children. Unfortunately, she died giving birth to their 14th child. The husband was so grieved at his terrible loss that he decided to build the tomb in her memory.

A leitura da versão (b) flui melhor devido ao emprego de pronomes e substantivos que se referem ao imperador, à imperatriz ou a ambos. Essa substituição lexical torna a leitura menos enfadonha e facilita a percepção da coesão textual.
